\textbf{Engagement integrity defense.}  To limit the impact of synthetic interaction attacks, two mechanisms are applied before engagement signals enter the EWMA/Bayesian update.

\emph{(i) Velocity clamp.}  Let $\bar{V}^{(t)} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} V_i^{(t)}$ be the per-round population mean of raw views.  For each image, the observed engagement is clipped:
\begin{equation}
V_i^{(t)} \leftarrow \min\!\bigl(V_i^{(t)},\; k\,\bar{V}^{(t)}\bigr), \qquad k = 2,
\end{equation}
with likes, watch time, and examinations scaled by the same ratio when clamping activates.  This bounds the per-round influence of any single image to at most $k$ times the population average.

\emph{(ii) Trust ramp.}  A newly uploaded image of age $a_i^{(t)} = t - t_i^{\mathrm{arr}}$ has its engagement signals attenuated by
\begin{equation}
\tau_i^{(t)} = \min\!\left(1,\; \frac{a_i^{(t)}}{M}\right), \qquad M = 200\text{ rounds},
\end{equation}
so that the effective per-round update becomes $\tilde{N}_i^{(t)} \leftarrow (1-\rho)\tilde{N}_i^{(t-1)} + \rho\,\tau_i^{(t)}\,N_i^{(t)}$.  During the maturity period ($a_i < M$), engagement accumulates slowly regardless of volume, preventing rapid rank inflation of newly injected content.  Once an image reaches age $M$, $\tau = 1$ and the standard update resumes.
